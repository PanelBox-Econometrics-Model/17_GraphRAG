# Resolucao BCB 4.893/2021 - Cybersecurity and Information Security Policy

Resolution 4.893 was enacted by the National Monetary Council (Conselho Monetario Nacional - CMN) on February 26, 2021, establishing the requirements for the cybersecurity policy and for contracting data processing, data storage, and cloud computing services by financial institutions and other institutions authorized to operate by the Banco Central do Brasil (BCB). This resolution supersedes Resolution 4.658/2018, consolidating and updating the regulatory framework for cybersecurity in the Brazilian financial sector.

## Scope and Applicability

As defined in Article 1, Resolution 4.893/2021 applies to all financial institutions and other entities authorized to operate by the Banco Central do Brasil. This includes commercial banks, investment banks, development banks, savings banks, credit unions, payment institutions, exchange brokerages, securities brokerages, credit financing and investment companies, mortgage companies, and other entities operating under BCB authorization.

Pursuant to Article 2, institutions classified as segments S1, S2, S3, S4, and S5 under the BCB prudential segmentation framework (Resolution 4.553/2017) are subject to the requirements, with proportionality applied according to the nature, size, complexity, structure, risk profile, and business model of each institution. Smaller institutions (S5 segment) may adopt simplified cybersecurity policies, but must still comply with the core obligations.

## Cybersecurity Policy Requirements

Under Articles 3 through 7, every institution must formulate and implement a cybersecurity policy that addresses, at a minimum, the following areas:

### Strategic Objectives

- The institution's objectives for information security and cybersecurity, aligned with its strategic planning and risk management framework.
- Procedures and controls adopted to reduce vulnerability to cybersecurity incidents, encompassing both internal systems and systems operated by third parties.
- Specific controls covering the authentication and traceability of access to information systems, including encryption and data protection mechanisms.

### Risk Assessment and Prevention

- Procedures for classifying data and information based on their relevance to the institution's business operations (Article 3, Paragraph II).
- Mechanisms for detection, prevention, and reduction of vulnerability to cybersecurity incidents, including procedures for security testing and network traffic analysis.
- Scenario analysis and stress testing related to cybersecurity events, covering threats to confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

### Awareness and Training

- Programs for dissemination of cybersecurity culture within the institution, including mandatory training for employees, contractors, and third-party service providers with access to sensitive systems (Article 3, Paragraph VII).

The cybersecurity policy must be approved by the board of directors or, in its absence, by the executive board of the institution, and must be reviewed at least annually (Article 5).

## Incident Response Plan

Articles 6 and 7 mandate that institutions establish and maintain a comprehensive incident response plan. This plan must include:

- Clear definition of what constitutes a relevant cybersecurity incident, including events that affect the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information.
- Procedures for identification, classification, registration, communication, and remediation of cybersecurity incidents.
- Designation of the area or team responsible for incident response and crisis management.
- Communication protocols for informing the board of directors, BCB, affected clients, and other relevant stakeholders.
- Post-incident analysis procedures, including root cause investigation and implementation of corrective measures.
- Periodic testing and simulation exercises to validate the effectiveness of the incident response plan (Article 7, Paragraph III).

## Cloud Computing and Data Processing Services

Resolution 4.893/2021 dedicates specific provisions (Articles 11 through 19) to the contracting of cloud computing services, data processing, and data storage by financial institutions. This section consolidates and refines the rules previously established under Resolution 4.658/2018.

### General Requirements

Institutions must ensure that the contracting of relevant data processing, data storage, and cloud computing services does not impair their ability to comply with regulatory obligations, including BCB access to data and information (Article 11).

Prior to contracting these services, institutions must verify that adequate security, governance, and contingency controls are in place (Article 12). The institution remains fully responsible for the security and integrity of the data, regardless of whether processing is performed by a third party.

### Domestic and Cross-Border Services

Article 15 establishes that services involving the processing, storage, or handling of data related to operations carried out in Brazil must be performed in the national territory, unless the institution demonstrates that equivalent levels of data protection and regulatory access can be maintained abroad.

For cross-border cloud computing services (Article 16), the institution must:

- Ensure that the BCB has access to data and information processed or stored abroad.
- Establish contractual provisions guaranteeing that data can be transferred to another service provider or repatriated to Brazil without disruption.
- Communicate to the BCB the countries and regions where data will be processed or stored.

## Contracting of Data Processing and Storage Services

Under Articles 12 through 14, institutions must adopt due diligence procedures prior to contracting third-party data processing and storage services. The contracting process must include:

- Assessment of the provider's capacity to ensure data security, confidentiality, and integrity.
- Verification of business continuity and disaster recovery capabilities.
- Contractual provisions granting the institution and the BCB access to data, information, and audit records.
- Provisions for data portability and orderly transition in case of termination of the service agreement.
- Clear definition of the provider's responsibility for compliance with Brazilian data protection regulations, including the Lei Geral de Protecao de Dados (LGPD - Law 13.709/2018).

## Requirements for Cloud Service Providers

Article 16 and Article 17 specify that cloud service providers contracted by financial institutions must comply with the following requirements:

- Maintain adequate security certifications recognized by the industry, such as ISO/IEC 27001, SOC 2, or equivalent standards.
- Provide transparency regarding the physical location of data centers and the jurisdictions where data is stored and processed.
- Guarantee segregation of the institution's data from data of other clients of the provider.
- Allow the institution and the BCB to conduct audits and inspections, either directly or through independent third parties.
- Implement encryption, access controls, and logging mechanisms consistent with the institution's cybersecurity policy.

The institution must maintain and regularly update a registry of all cloud computing services contracted, including provider identification, nature of services, data classification, and geographic location of data processing (Article 18).

## Board of Directors Responsibilities

Article 8 establishes that the board of directors (or the executive board, in the absence of a board of directors) is responsible for:

- Approving the cybersecurity policy and ensuring its alignment with the institution's risk appetite and strategic objectives.
- Designating a qualified director responsible for the implementation and enforcement of the cybersecurity policy (Article 8, Paragraph I). This director may also serve as the chief information security officer (CISO), depending on the institution's governance structure.
- Overseeing the institution's cybersecurity posture through regular reports and key risk indicators.
- Ensuring adequate allocation of resources (financial, technological, and human) for cybersecurity initiatives.

## Information Security Governance

Articles 8 through 10 require institutions to adopt an information security governance framework that includes:

- A formal organizational structure with clearly defined roles and responsibilities for cybersecurity and information security management.
- Integration of cybersecurity risk management into the institution's overall risk management framework (Article 9).
- Internal and external audit processes covering cybersecurity controls, including penetration testing and vulnerability assessments.
- Metrics and key performance indicators (KPIs) for monitoring the effectiveness of cybersecurity controls.
- Documentation and record-keeping of all cybersecurity-related decisions, incidents, and corrective actions for a minimum period of five years (Article 10, Paragraph II).

## Data Protection Requirements

Resolution 4.893/2021 establishes data protection requirements that are complementary to the Lei Geral de Protecao de Dados (LGPD). In particular:

- Institutions must implement data classification systems that categorize information based on sensitivity and criticality (Article 3, Paragraph II).
- Personal data of clients and employees must be protected through encryption, anonymization, or pseudonymization techniques where applicable.
- Access to sensitive data must be restricted based on the principle of least privilege and subject to continuous monitoring and logging.
- Data retention and disposal policies must be established and communicated to all relevant stakeholders.
- Cross-border data transfers must comply with both BCB regulations and LGPD requirements, including ensuring adequate levels of protection in the destination jurisdiction.

## Reporting Obligations to BCB

Articles 20 through 22 establish the following reporting obligations:

- Institutions must report relevant cybersecurity incidents to the BCB within the timeframes established in supplementary regulations (currently governed by BCB Circular 3.979/2020 and subsequent normative acts).
- Annual reports on the implementation and effectiveness of the cybersecurity policy must be submitted to the BCB.
- Any material changes to the cybersecurity policy, the incident response plan, or the contracting of data processing and cloud computing services must be communicated to the BCB promptly.
- The designated director must present an annual report to the board of directors summarizing cybersecurity events, risk assessments, and improvement actions taken during the period (Article 22).

## Penalties and Enforcement

Non-compliance with Resolution 4.893/2021 may result in administrative sanctions under the terms of Law 13.506/2017 and BCB normative acts. Penalties may include:

- Formal warnings and reprimands.
- Monetary fines, which may be imposed on both the institution and individually on directors and officers responsible for the cybersecurity area.
- Temporary or permanent prohibition from holding management positions in financial institutions.
- Restriction, suspension, or cancellation of the institution's operating authorization in severe cases.

The BCB may also require institutions to submit remediation plans and impose additional supervisory measures, including enhanced reporting requirements and mandatory independent audits.

## Transition Timeline (from Resolution 4.658/2018 to Resolution 4.893/2021)

Resolution 4.893/2021 entered into force on July 1, 2021, repealing Resolution 4.658/2018 in its entirety (Article 26). The transition provisions established the following:

- Institutions that were already in compliance with Resolution 4.658/2018 were considered provisionally in compliance with Resolution 4.893/2021, subject to adapting their policies and procedures to any new or modified requirements within 180 days.
- Contracts for data processing, storage, and cloud computing services entered into under Resolution 4.658/2018 remained valid, but institutions were required to review and update contractual clauses to align with the new requirements at the earliest renewal or amendment opportunity.
- Institutions in the process of implementing the original requirements under Resolution 4.658/2018 were required to complete implementation in accordance with the deadlines and conditions set forth in Resolution 4.893/2021, using the updated framework as the reference standard.

The consolidation of the regulatory framework under Resolution 4.893/2021 aimed to simplify compliance obligations, address gaps identified during the application of Resolution 4.658/2018, and incorporate lessons learned from cybersecurity incidents in the Brazilian financial sector between 2018 and 2021.
